## Karl – Insight Agent<br/>User Manual

## 1\. Overview

In today’s modern data landscape, enterprises operate across heterogeneous data estates — spanning cloud data platforms, on-premises systems, SaaS applications, and governed analytics layers. While cloud ecosystems such as Microsoft Azure provide robust foundational services for ingestion, transformation, and analytics, organizations increasingly require an intelligence layer that abstracts complexity and enables business users to interact with data intuitively, securely, and at scale.

**Karl – Insight Agent**, developed by Kanerika Inc., is designed to serve as that intelligence layer.

## 1.1 Karl – Insight Agent

_Karl – Insight Agent_ is an enterprise-grade AI analytics platform that enables natural language–driven data analysis, visualization, and insights generation over governed enterprise data. The system is designed with a modular, secure, and scalable architecture, leveraging Microsoft Fabric as the central data and analytics backbone. It integrates:

*   **Natural Language–driven analytics orchestration** using large language models for intent understanding and reasoning.
*   **Semantic retrieval and context-aware processing** to enrich user queries with relevant data and historical context.
*   **Dynamic workflow orchestration**, where the backend determines the appropriate execution path based on user intent.

Each action consumes trial credits. A credits status indicator is visible within KARL UI so users understand how much trial capacity remains.

## 3.3 Trial Capabilities and Limits

During the 30‑day trial, you have full access to the tool’s features, subject to the following limits:

| Capability | Trial Limit |
| --- | --- |
| Duration | 30 days from the moment you start the trial |
| Credits Limit | 5100 Credits (included in free trial) |

These limits allow you to validate the tool against real workloads while ensuring fair usage during evaluation. If you attempt to put queries after exhaustion of 5100 credits, you will be prompted to upgrade.

## 3.4 Trial Duration and Notifications

The tool keeps you informed of your trial status through clear visual indicators:

*   **Home screen banner** – Shows the number of days remaining in your trial (e.g., “14 days left for trial”).

_![](/images/extracted/img_5b0c2dc0.png)_

*   **Authentication screen banner** – When fewer than five days remain, a reminder keeps appearing on the main configuration page, along with an **Upgrade Now** option.

These notifications ensure you are never surprised by the trial ending and can plan your upgrade accordingly.

## 3.5 Upgrading from the Trial

When your trial credits exhaust or you need to continue after the 30‑day period, upgrading is straightforward.

*   **Conversion path:** Click **Upgrade** from any of the Karl Home page. You will be taken to the Azure Marketplace listing for the Karl – Insight Agent, where you can select a paid plan.

![](/images/extracted/img_2b26bee8.png)

*   **Payment options:** You can pay by credit card or link the subscription to your Azure subscription (invoiced through Azure).
*   **Seamless transition:** After completing the purchase, return to your Fabric workspace. The tool automatically detects the active subscription, removes the trial limits, and displays the licensed version.

## 3.6 What Happens After the Trial Ends?

If your trial expires (either by reaching 30 days or by hitting credit limit) without an upgrade:

*   You will no longer be able to execute new migrations.
*   Existing insights will still remain available, but **new query generation** is disabled.
*   A dialog prompts you to upgrade, clearly explaining the next steps.

You can always choose to remove the trial artifacts or contact our support team for assistance.

We hope this trial gives you the confidence to adopt the Karl – Insight Agent for your production workloads. If you have any questions during your evaluation, please reach out to our support portal at [**Support Portal**](https://kanerika.com/datasheet-page/kar-data-intelligence-agent/) .

## **6.2 Service Level Agreement**

### ****6.2.1** **Priority Level Definitions****

**Kanerika utilizes a tiered incident response logic to address technical urgency and business impact within the Microsoft Fabric environment.**

*   ****P0 – Critical: Total System Outage.** A complete failure that prevents all users from accessing the workload or blocks all analysis capabilities.
    
    *   _Example 1:_ A total failure of the Fabric Workload SDK integration, preventing the Karl interface from loading within the Fabric portal.
    *   _Example 2:_ A complete failure of the **Usage & Subscription DB (PostgreSQL RDS)**, which prevents the credit system from authorizing any user operations.
    
    **
*   ****P1 – High: Major Functionality Degradation.** Critical features are unavailable to multiple users with no viable workaround.
    
    *   _Example 1:_ Failure of the **Deep Analysis** or **Why Analysis** modules, halting high-resource, credit-intensive diagnostic operations.
    *   _Example 2:_ Synchronization failures between the Azure Marketplace subscription and the Fabric environment, causing incorrect credit exhaustion errors.
    
    **
*   ****P2 – Medium: Partial Disruption.** Functional errors exist, but a viable workaround allows the user to continue activities.
    
    *   _Example 1:_ Errors in specific Plotly chart generations where the user can still access the raw data via the "Technical View."
    *   _Example 2:_ Intermittent latency in SQL generation that can be resolved by re-submitting the natural language query.
    
    **
*   ****P3 – Low: General Inquiries.** Minor usability issues, documentation clarifications, or enhancement requests.
    
    *   _Example 1:_ Clarification requests regarding training data CRUD operations.
    *   _Example 2:_ Suggestions for UI/UX cosmetic improvements.
    
    **

### **6.2.2 Response Time Targets and Performance Commitments**

**In cloud-native environments, time-bound acknowledgments are necessary to maintain enterprise trust. Kanerika commits to the following initial response time targets:**

| Priority Level | Initial Response Time |
| --- | --- |
| **P0 – Critical** | Within 4 hours |
| **P1 – High** | Within 8 hours |
| **P2 – Medium** | Within 24 hours |
| **P3 – Low** | Within 4 business days |

****Definition of Response Time:** The duration required for Kanerika support personnel to acknowledge receipt of the ticket, assess the technical severity, and initiate a formal investigation.**

****External Dependencies:** While Kanerika strives for rapid resolution, resolution times are subject to external variables. These include **Azure OpenAI GPT-4 rate limits**, **OneLake availability**, and Microsoft Fabric capacity constraints**
